CLIENT REPORT

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?
I like a natural, minimalist look, so I looked at websites with a similar vibe to help decide the type of layout I was after. I also looked at other reed diffuser websites to see the kind of content and images that was used to get a feel of how I would create the same on my site. All images on my site were AI generated, which helped produce the type of professional but inviting look.

How did you communicate with your developer?
Via teams and purely in chat mode.

Was there any issues with communication?
It was very spotty communication wise. I would reach out to the developer quite often throughout the week, but would take between 1-4 days to hear from them. This was hard, because we're in different timezones where I'm a day ahead. The assignments are due on my Sunday, so I would try to hand them in on my Saturday. The developer would try to contact me on my Sunday, I am not contactable. While I tried to set up a weekly Teams call, I received no response to advise availability so I could set a day and time. I was always clear when reaching out with questions or seeking confirmation by stipulating a time when I needed a response by. Unfortunately, more of than not, I would receive a response after this time or nothing at all.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?
Yes, he did well in developing the site. There were a couple of places where the wireframe was not followed and corrected him in one area (the image to use for the 'Shop Collection' on the home page). Overall, am happy with the end result.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?
Push the importance of communication from the start. Not sure there was much else to do differently, as I feel I did all that I could to make the most of the situation where Tyler and I were only a 2 person group.

LEAD DEVELOPER REPORT

For this project, I was both the Lead and Junior Developer. While we were originally a team of five, only one of them responded in time, in which we agreed we would be each others client and work by ourselves on each others sites.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?
Via teams chat. One of the things I asked from the client was to provide better resolution images. I received better images for the "Projects" page, but never received received a response or any updated images for the 'About Me' page.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client? The week of HTML was good. The week of CSS was a little more difficult as for some reason the site wouldn't update once the CSS had been written. Thankfully, after waiting a few minutes and tinkering around, I clicked on the link in the 'Pages' section and it took me to the updated site and I was able to share this with the client.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub? GitHub was a real learning curve (still is!) as I'd never used it before. I had to really dissect the instructions from Brother Hancock and make sure I was following the right steps. However, it was great to be able to push the changes whenever I made any updates to the files in VS Code. This is a super handy feature.

GENERAL EXPERIENCE AND REFLECTIONS

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?
Communication was the most challenging part. The lack of communication started from the very beginning of contact my group members. It was nice to know that I wasn't the only one expereiencing this issue as there were also a couple of other students from different groups not being able to establish contact with their team members.

Yes, I was able to overcome this when Tyler and I agreed to be eachothers clients and also be the Lead and Junior Developers on each others sites.

What did you enjoy most about this project?
I actually really enjoyed creating the website for the client. It really did make it feel like I had a client that I was working for with deadlines to meet certain requirements. I think if we had the full team, this would have been an awesome project to do with each person experiencing a different role and getting to see the dynamic between lead and junior developers.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?
Again, communication with the team and make it known from the very beginning how important it is. Especially when there are deadlines to meet.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.
I definitely feel more confident after this project to see what might happen in the real world. From the prospect of working in a small team with each member having a clear role and how they contribute the site production. It also introduced me to GitHub, which I would assume would be similar to other platforms used professionally. I'm also glad we got to create our site plans and wireframes as this will also help understand the initial stages of developing a website. This really was a great way to close out this class.
